Soft Manipulator for Agricultural Purposes

The Soft Manipulator for Agricultural Purposes project focused on creating a robotic system capable of selectively harvesting ripe tomatoes using advanced image processing techniques. The system was designed to address challenges in agricultural automation, such as minimizing damage to delicate produce while maintaining efficiency.

At the heart of the system was a custom-designed soft manipulator with three flexible silicone fingers. These soft fingers provided a gentle yet firm grip, ensuring the tomatoes were picked without bruising or damage. The robot utilized image processing algorithms to identify and distinguish ripe tomatoes based on their color and size, ensuring accurate and selective harvesting.

The use of advanced image processing, implemented via Raspberry Pi, allowed for real-time decision-making and adaptability in dynamic agricultural environments. This innovative solution demonstrated the potential of soft robotics in improving the efficiency, precision, and sustainability of farming operations, marking a significant step toward smarter, technology-driven agriculture.
