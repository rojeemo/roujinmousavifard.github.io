Design, Fabrication, and Control of Robotic Systems

This project showcased the design, fabrication, and control of multiple robotic systems, each tailored for distinct industrial and automation applications. The endeavor was divided into three main phases:

Color Detection and Sorting with a Robotic Arm: A 3-degree-of-freedom robotic arm was designed and built, equipped with a gripper for object manipulation. The system integrated sensors and algorithms to identify objects based on their color. Once detected, the robotic arm sorted the products and placed them in designated locations, ensuring high precision and efficiency. This application is particularly suited for assembly lines and sorting tasks in manufacturing industries.

Dynamic Tracking Robotic Arm: The second phase involved the development of a more advanced robotic arm capable of tracking and following a specific item in real time. Using a camera and image processing algorithms, the robotic arm dynamically adjusted its movements to maintain alignment with the target. This functionality is ideal for applications such as object inspection, quality assurance, and interactive robotics in dynamic environments.

Line-Tracking Mobile Robot: The final phase focused on designing and programming a robot capable of moving in a straight line. This system was developed to demonstrate a path-planning method. Such a feature is essential for applications like material transportation in warehouses or predefined path navigation in industrial settings.
